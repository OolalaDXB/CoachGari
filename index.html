What happens next

Three phases, in order.

Whichever route you pick, the path is the same. Nothing in a later phase starts until the one before it has told us something real.

P1Weeks 1–4

Get it live and find out what people pay

Site up, link in your bio, every enquiry through one channel so we can count them.

  • Site live, WhatsApp and form connected
  • Bio, link and video covers rewritten
  • Quote a real price to your next twenty enquiries and see who says yes
  • We log where they are, what they asked for, what they'd pay
P2Weeks 5–12

Turn payment on

Prices set from what P1 showed us, not from guessing. Collection is handled on my side, so there's nothing for you to set up.

  • Stripe connected for card payments, prices published
  • Mobile money added where cards don't reach — EcoCash and equivalents
  • Different pricing by region, so Southern Africa can actually buy
  • First live group cohort opened
  • First three named testimonials collected and published
P3From month four

Turn it into something people belong to

Only worth doing once there are paying clients to look after.

  • Member accounts on an Oo page — one login, your sessions, their progress
  • Recurring cohorts instead of one-off sales
  • Dates in Zimbabwe and on the road, sold in advance
  • Nutrition extended into products — sports lines and dehydrated food, sold alongside the coaching rather than as a separate errand
  • Brand partnerships once the following is big enough to justify them

Zimbabwe & Southern Africa

Dubai pays now.
Home compounds.

You've said growing the audience back home matters to you, and I think you're right to hold onto it — but the two markets don't run on the same clock. Dubai pays this month, at Dubai rates. Zimbabwe builds something that won't pay much for a while and then pays for years. Go in expecting the first and you'll quit by Christmas. Go in knowing it's a three-year build funded by the Dubai work, and you'll still be standing when nobody else is.

01

Volume, not premium

Five hundred people at ten dollars beats twenty at eighty. Growing the audience only becomes a business if the cheap group format exists — which is why it's in Route C.

02

Payment comes before content

Stripe handles cards. Cards don't reach most of your audience. EcoCash and mobile money are the real rail, and it's the first thing to solve — not the last.

03

The diaspora is the bridge

Zimbabweans in South Africa, the UK, Canada and Australia follow home creators and pay in hard currency. Content aimed at home reaches them for free. Same sessions, two prices.

04

Padel is your open door

Certified, already Head of Padel in Gaborone, and the sport is growing fast across the region. Nobody has taken the seat of the padel voice for Southern Africa yet.

05

Speak from there, not about there

Post on Harare time. Training with no equipment, power cuts, what food actually costs at the market. Your best videos today are filmed in Dubai for everyone — these would be for them.

06

One date in Harare

An in-person session converts what six months of posting can't. It proves you come back, it turns followers into faces, and it films itself.
